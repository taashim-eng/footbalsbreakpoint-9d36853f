# External overlay facts (cited) — for the adversarial review

All figures below are **publicly reported** and used only as qualitative context.
No per-match gambling, revenue, or corruption values exist in our dataset; none
are fabricated or joined to match records.

## FIFA financial incentive (motive, not evidence)
- FIFA 2019–2022 cycle revenue: **USD 7.57 bn** (record); TV broadcasting rights
  **USD 3.43 bn = 45%**; Qatar 2022 alone contributed 83% of cycle revenue.
  Marketing rights USD 1.80 bn. — FIFA Annual Report 2019–2022; SportBusiness.
- 2026 (48 teams / 104 matches, US-Canada-Mexico): FIFA projects **USD 11–13 bn**
  revenue; **USD 4.26 bn** from broadcasting alone (+43% vs 2022). — Finance Football.
- Reading: large financial upside when big-market / host teams advance ⇒ a *motive*
  exists. Motive is not evidence of on-pitch manipulation.

## Gambling scale
- 2022 World Cup wagers: estimates range **~USD 35 bn (regulated)** to **>USD 100–160 bn**
  incl. unregulated. UK bookmakers ~£2.5 bn; US ~USD 1.8 bn. — Forbes 2022; CNBC.
- Regulated global sports-betting market ~**USD 113.8 bn**; UNODC estimates the
  **illegal** market up to **USD 1.7 trillion**. — UNODC via SCMP / Al Majalla.
- Reading: fixing incentives concentrate in the *unregulated* market and in *spot*
  markets (props), not typically in outright World-Cup final results.

## Proven corruption is administrative, not on-pitch results
- 2015 FIFA case (US DOJ): 47-count indictment, ~USD 150 m in bribes over two decades,
  centred on **media & marketing-rights** for tournaments (e.g. Copa América);
  racketeering / wire fraud / money laundering. Officials Webb, Warner, Blazer et al.
  Blatter & Platini were FIFA-suspended but **acquitted of criminal fraud** by a Swiss
  court (2022). — Wikipedia "2015 FIFA corruption case"; US DOJ.
- Reading: the marquee corruption concerns **money/administration** (bids, rights,
  elections), not manipulation of the on-pitch results in our sample.

## Proven match-fixing sits in qualifiers / lower tiers / officials
- Nepal players arrested over 2011 WC **qualifier** fixing (2015); referee Ibrahim
  Chaibou life-banned (2019); Samson Siasia life ban; several African internationals
  life-banned for "match manipulation" (2019); China ref Lu Jun jailed. — Wikipedia
  "Match fixing in association football".
- Reading: documented fixing is overwhelmingly **not** World-Cup-finals matches
  (our 2002–2022 sample), and often targets **referees / specific incidents**.

## 2026 integrity concerns are spot-fixing & officiating, not result-fixing
- Ivorian player **Elye Wahi** was initially refused entry to Canada over an *alleged*
  spot-fixing matter (later allowed to travel). Independent integrity experts referred
  ≥2 World Cup players to national federations over suspected **spot-fixing**
  (manipulating cards / corners / throw-ins — not the final result). A FIFA Disciplinary
  Committee statement was issued 6 July 2026. Several officiating controversies logged
  (incl. Argentina, Qatar–Switzerland, England–Ghana). — Wikipedia "List of 2026 FIFA
  World Cup controversies"; FIFA media; Yahoo/France24.
- Reading: modern documented risk is **spot-fixing** (in-game props) and refereeing —
  both **invisible to a final-score / goals analysis** like ours. Allegations (e.g.
  Egypt's complaint about Argentina's Round of 16 decisions) are unproven and cannot be
  adjudicated from scorelines.

## Net implication for the report
Our analysis speaks only to **result manipulation expressed through goals**. It is
silent on spot-fixing, refereeing, and administrative corruption — which is precisely
where the *documented* integrity failures and the *strongest financial incentives* live.
That is a scope limitation to state plainly, not a reason to infer wrongdoing.
